## Objective

INTERACT aims to lay the foundation for intuitive multi-robot interaction in human-centered environments.

According to the CORDIS fact sheet, the project is meant to make it possible for teams of mobile robots to interact safely among humans and to enable a new level of automation in factories and cities.

The project keywords exposed in the fact sheet are also revealing: multi-robot systems, motion planning, dynamic vehicle routing, and intelligent transportation. So this is not only a social-robotics project in the conversational sense, but a systems project about how robot teams move and coordinate around people in real shared spaces.

## Relevance to the Vault

This grant is not a racing grant, but it is a useful neighboring branch for the vault because it stresses a different version of autonomy under consequence:

- not only speed,
- not only control near the limit,
- but safe, intuitive, socially compatible robot behavior in human environments.

Its main contribution to the vault is conceptual:

- it strengthens the `autonomy among humans` perspective,
- it connects motion planning to human-centered interaction rather than only physical feasibility,
- and it pushes the vault toward a richer view of robot competence where prediction, coordination, and intelligibility matter alongside raw control.

## Notes

The important thing about INTERACT for this vault is that it broadens the notion of robustness.

In many pages here, robustness means handling speed, uncertainty, terrain, or hardware faults. INTERACT adds another dimension: robots may also need to be robust to dense shared human environments, where failure is not only a control breakdown but also unintuitive or unsafe interaction with people.

That makes it a good anchor for any future expansion of the vault toward human-aware planning, multi-robot coordination in public space, or interaction-aware autonomy.
