# Artificial General Intelligence

## Definition

Artificial general intelligence is the idea of a machine intelligence that can generalize across tasks, contexts, and problem types with a breadth and adaptability closer to human intelligence than to narrow task-specific systems.

In this vault, AGI matters less as a marketing label and more as a question about what kind of system architecture could support genuinely broad competence.

## Key Framing

[[agi_imagined_how_is_agi_configured_by_the_theories_of_mind]] argues that AGI is never imagined from nowhere. It is configured by implicit theories of mind.

[[why_ai_systems_dont_learn_and_what_to_do_about_it_lessons_on_autonomous_learning_from_cognitive_science]] adds a more learning-theoretic criticism of the same space: a system may look broadly capable while still lacking the internal structure required for genuine [[autonomous_learning]].

[[ai_and_human_co_improvement_for_safer_co_superintelligence]] adds a relational criticism: even if highly capable AI arrives, the important question may be whether AGI is built into a cooperative human-AI co-improvement regime or a more one-sided replacement regime.

That means different AGI visions lean toward different assumptions:

- a computational mind picture that privileges representation, abstraction, and reasoning,
- or an embodied mind picture that privileges situated action, environment coupling, and lived interaction.

This framing is useful because it explains why AGI debates so often recur inside the vault's other branches without always naming themselves as AGI debates.

## Relationship to the Vault

This topic sits naturally near [[foundation_models_for_intelligent_decision_making]], [[embodied_autonomous_intelligence]], and [[era_of_experience]].

The foundation-model branch raises the question of whether broad pretrained multimodal priors can become the substrate for general intelligence.

The embodied-intelligence branch raises the question of whether intelligence without body, viability constraints, and environmental coupling is missing something fundamental.

The experience branch raises the question of whether true generality requires not only broad prior knowledge, but also ongoing self-generated learning from grounded interaction.

## Open Problems

Open questions include whether AGI should be modeled primarily as scalable reasoning, scalable grounded interaction, or some hybrid; how much embodiment is actually necessary for generality; whether foundation models can become genuinely general without persistent experience; and how broad intelligence should be evaluated once symbolic fluency and embodied competence diverge.
