# AI Wargaming

## Definition

AI wargaming studies how AI systems behave when placed inside structured strategic-conflict simulations used to explore escalation, deterrence, military planning, and crisis response.

The core issue is not only whether the system can produce plausible actions, but whether its strategic tendencies are safe, stable, interpretable, and aligned with human judgment in high-stakes domains.

## Key Framing

[[human_vs_machine_behavioral_differences_between_expert_humans_and_language_models_in_wargame_simulations]] frames wargaming as a way to compare expert-human and foundation-model decision behavior under escalation pressure.

The key finding is that coarse agreement can hide important finer-grained differences in aggression, sensitivity to framing, and internal decision quality.

## Relationship to Strategic AI

This topic sits near [[real_time_strategy_games]] and [[foundation_models_for_intelligent_decision_making]].

RTS games are useful as technical benchmarks for strategic coordination and long-horizon consequences, but AI wargaming adds a more human and policy-sensitive question: whether the strategic behavior itself is appropriate in settings where error can carry severe real-world implications.

## Open Problems

Open problems include how to evaluate strategic-model behavior beyond surface plausibility, how to represent disagreement and dissent inside simulated teams, how to test robustness across scenarios and prompting variations, and whether foundation models should ever be trusted with recommendation roles in escalation-sensitive settings.
