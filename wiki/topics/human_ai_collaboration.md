# Human-AI Collaboration

## Definition

Human-AI collaboration studies how humans and AI systems can be combined so that each complements the other's strengths and limitations rather than one merely imitating or replacing the other.

In this vault, the topic matters because many autonomy questions are not only about whether AI can act alone, but about when human judgment should remain central, when AI should assist, and how humans should understand the systems they work with.

## Key Framing

[[human_versus_artificial_intelligence]] argues that human intelligence should not automatically be treated as the gold standard that AI must duplicate. Human and artificial intelligence may differ in their constraints, generality, and cognitive style, and those differences create an opportunity for complementarity rather than only competition.

[[ai_and_human_co_improvement_for_safer_co_superintelligence]] pushes that framing much further. It argues that under very high capability, the central problem may not be imitation or replacement, but whether humans and AI can improve together in a way that remains safe and cooperative.

[[cognitio_emergens_agency_dimensions_and_dynamics_in_human_ai_knowledge_co_creation]] adds a more fine-grained internal view of collaboration itself. Instead of asking only whether humans and AI complement one another in the abstract, it asks how agency is distributed and how knowledge is jointly produced during real interaction.

That framing shifts the question from:

- "when will AI become human-like?"

toward:

- "what kinds of intelligence do humans and AI each provide, and how should they be combined?"

## Open Problems

Open questions include how humans form mental models of AI collaborators, how much authority should be delegated under uncertainty, how to preserve agency without sacrificing safety or performance, and how to evaluate complementarity rather than only replacement.
