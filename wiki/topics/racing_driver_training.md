# Racing Driver Training

## Definition

Racing driver training studies how human drivers can improve lap-time performance, corner technique, and safety through structured feedback, data analysis, coaching, or simulation-based instruction.

## Key Framing

[[data_driven_driver_training_via_counterfactual_and_language_based_guidance_in_racing_scenarios]] frames the problem as personalized machine coaching for novice drivers in racing scenarios.

Its central idea is that driver training should not only say whether a corner was good or bad. It should explain which feature-level behavioral changes would most effectively move a driver toward expert-level performance, then express those changes in language the driver can act on.

This makes racing driver training an interesting adjacent branch of the vault: instead of teaching the autonomous system to race directly, it teaches humans with the help of data-driven models, explainability tools, and language feedback.

## Relationship to Driver Expertise and Simulation

This topic belongs near [[professional_race_driver_expertise]] and [[autonomous_racing_simulation]].

Expertise research asks what elite drivers do. Driver-training systems ask how to transfer some of that expertise to less experienced drivers in a personalized and scalable way.

Simulation matters because it allows repeated corner practice, controlled comparisons, and lower-risk feedback loops before any transfer to physical driving.

## Open Problems

Open problems include real-world transfer from simulation, avoiding overreliance on proxy features, adapting feedback to different learning styles, and deciding how much instruction should be descriptive, predictive, or counterfactual.
