# Driver Reaction Time

## Definition

Driver reaction time is the delay between perceiving a relevant event and producing a control response such as braking, steering, or throttle adjustment.

In this vault, the topic matters because reaction time forms part of the baseline against which high-speed autonomous systems and expert drivers are often implicitly compared.

## Key Framing

[[evaluation_of_driver_reaction_time]] studies this issue in a driving-simulator setting and emphasizes how reaction time changes across driver attributes and impairment conditions.

That framing is useful because it reminds the vault that human driving competence is not only about skill and strategy. It is also constrained by a biological perception-action loop whose timing can worsen under stress, inexperience, or alcohol.

## Relationship to the Vault

This topic sits near [[professional_race_driver_expertise]], [[Highly_dynamic_autonomous_system]], and [[high_speed_perception]].

In racing and agile autonomy, raw reaction time is not the whole story; expert anticipation and better state estimation can partly compensate for slower reflexes. But reaction time still remains one of the hard background constraints that shape both human and machine performance.

## Open Problems

Open questions include how much elite anticipation reduces dependence on raw reflex, how simulator-measured reaction times transfer to real high-speed driving, and how human reaction-time limits should be compared fairly with machine perception-and-control latency.
