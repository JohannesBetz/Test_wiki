# Autonomous Vehicle Safety

## Definition

Autonomous vehicle safety concerns how autonomous road vehicles behave under real operational conditions, especially when compared against human driving in terms of accidents, claims, reliability, and risk.

In this vault, the topic matters as a road-going counterpart to the racing and high-dynamics branches: instead of asking whether a system is fast, the question is whether it is safe and how that safety should be measured.

## Key Framing

[[do_autonomous_vehicles_outperform_latest_generation_human_driven_vehicles_a_comparison_to_waymos_auto_liability_insurance_claims_at_25_million_miles]] frames this question through large-scale claims-based comparison between Waymo autonomous vehicles and human-driven vehicles.

That is useful because it shifts comparison away from anecdote and toward deployment-scale evidence.

## Relationship to the Vault

This topic sits near [[cognitive_navigation]], [[human_versus_artificial_intelligence]], and the broader human-versus-machine comparison branch.

It is also a useful counterweight to [[autonomous_vehicle_racing]]: racing asks how close a machine can get to the dynamic limit, while autonomous-vehicle safety asks how well an autonomous system behaves under ordinary but safety-critical public-road conditions.

## Open Problems

Open questions include how to compare AVs fairly to humans, which operational metrics best capture meaningful safety, how deployment context changes the interpretation of claims data, and how safety evidence should be integrated with broader discussions of trust, responsibility, and human-AI complementarity.
