# Korteling Human vs AI Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Korteling_HumanVSAI.pdf`
> Paper: Human- versus Artificial Intelligence, Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence, 2021

## Summary

This paper is a conceptual comparison of human intelligence and artificial intelligence. Its central claim is that AI should not be judged only by how closely it mimics human cognition, because human intelligence is only one possible form of general intelligence and AI systems may develop very different but still valuable cognitive profiles.

For this vault, the paper is most useful as a framing document for how human and artificial intelligence differ, how they can complement one another, and why human-AI collaboration may matter more than human imitation alone.

## Core Argument

The paper structures its contribution around three broad ideas:

- both human and artificial intelligence operate under fundamental constraints,
- human intelligence is only one variant of possible general intelligence,
- and future impact will likely come from multiple integrated forms of narrow or hybrid AI rather than one neat human-copying system.

This is a helpful corrective to overly anthropomorphic AI narratives.

## Why It Matters Here

The vault increasingly asks some version of the same question:

- should AI aim to replicate human cognition,
- should it complement human weaknesses instead,
- and how should humans develop usable mental models of AI partners?

This paper gives a clean conceptual scaffold for those questions and strengthens the bridge between [[foundation_models_for_intelligent_decision_making]], [[ai_agents_for_autonomous_systems]], and the broader human-machine comparison branch.

## Notes / Critique

This is a framing paper, not a robotics or racing methods paper. Its value lies in clarifying assumptions about intelligence, complementarity, and collaboration rather than proposing a concrete control or learning algorithm.
