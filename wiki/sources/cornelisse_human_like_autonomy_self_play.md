# Cornelisse Human-Like Autonomy Self-Play Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Cornelisse_Human-like autonomy emerges from self-play.pdf`
> Paper: Human-like autonomy emerges from self-play and a pinch of human data

## Summary

This source presents a driving-autonomy paper that asks a very specific and timely question:

can `human-like` autonomous behavior emerge mainly from self-play, with only a small amount of human data added as guidance?

That makes it a strong fit for the vault's growing branch around:

- human-versus-machine comparison,
- professional driver expertise,
- self-play and experience-driven learning,
- and what it really means for machine behavior to look `human-like` rather than merely fast or safe.

## Core Contribution

From the recoverable PDF metadata, the paper's framing is clear:

- the main setting is driving autonomy,
- the central mechanism is [[reinforcement_learning]] through self-play,
- and the novelty is the claim that a `pinch` of human data can steer the resulting policy toward more human-like behavior.

For this vault, that makes the paper especially useful as a bridge between:

- purely self-play or self-generated experience,
- imitation or behavior-cloning intuitions,
- and the broader question of whether human-like racing or driving style can be induced rather than hand-coded.
