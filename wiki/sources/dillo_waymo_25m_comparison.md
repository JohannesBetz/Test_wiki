# Dillo Waymo 25M Comparison Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Dillo_Comparison of Waymo and Human-Driven Vehicles at 25M_miles.pdf`
> Paper: Do Autonomous Vehicles Outperform Latest-Generation Human-Driven Vehicles? A Comparison to Waymo's Auto Liability Insurance Claims at 25 Million Miles, 2024

## Summary

This paper compares autonomous-vehicle safety performance against human-driven vehicles using Waymo's auto liability insurance claims after 25 million miles of autonomous driving.

For this vault, the paper is useful because it offers a large-scale real-world human-versus-autonomy comparison outside motorsport. Instead of asking who is faster or more agile, it asks who appears safer under a claims-based operational comparison.

## Core Contribution

The paper frames AV comparison through real deployment outcomes rather than simulated benchmarks or isolated showcase scenarios. That makes it a valuable complement to the vault's racing-focused human-versus-machine papers.

Its main significance is evaluative:

- how should autonomous performance be compared to humans at deployment scale,
- what kind of evidence counts as meaningful,
- and what does a claims-based comparison reveal about real-world safety rather than laboratory capability?

## Why It Matters Here

This source broadens the vault's human-machine comparison branch beyond racing and wargaming. It helps connect:

## Notes / Critique

This is a real-world deployment-comparison paper, not a high-dynamics control paper. Its value lies in comparative evidence and framing rather than in control architecture or learning methods.
