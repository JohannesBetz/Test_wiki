# Ferrag LLM and AI Agents for Autonomous Systems Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Ferrag_LLM_and_AI_Agents_for_Autonomous_Systems_A_Survey_of_Applications_Datasets_and_Security_Challenges.pdf`
> Paper: LLM and AI Agents for Autonomous Systems: A Survey of Applications, Datasets, and Security Challenges, IEEE OJITS 2026

## Summary

This paper surveys how large language models and AI agents are being used in autonomous systems, with explicit attention to datasets and security challenges.

For this vault, it matters because it widens the foundation-model conversation beyond robotics deployment alone. It brings in autonomous driving, UAV communication, benchmarks, and adversarial/security concerns under one survey frame.

## Method

The paper is a survey organized around three linked axes:

- applications,
- datasets and benchmarks,
- and security challenges.

That makes it useful as a systems-level map. Rather than asking only whether LLMs or agents are capable, it asks where they are being inserted into autonomous systems and what new failure modes appear once they become part of operational autonomy pipelines.

## Evaluation

The paper appears in the IEEE Open Journal of Intelligent Transportation Systems and explicitly covers both application opportunities and security risks.

For this vault, the key contribution is breadth with caution: it helps connect agentic autonomy enthusiasm to the less glamorous but essential questions of robustness, attack surface, and deployment trust.

## Notes / Critique

The strongest contribution is the security-aware framing. Many foundation-model surveys emphasize capability and transfer; this one also foregrounds the cost of giving agentic models more authority inside autonomous systems.

It belongs naturally near [[foundation_models_for_intelligent_decision_making]], [[ai_agents_for_autonomous_systems]], and the broader discussion of whether high-level agent competence can be made reliable enough for safety-critical autonomy.
