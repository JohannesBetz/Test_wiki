# Yoshida Embodied Autonomous Agents Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Yoshida_Embodied_Autonomous_Agents.pdf`
> Paper: Machine Survival: A Historical Perspective on Embodied Autonomous Intelligence, IOP Conference Series: Materials Science and Engineering, 2026

## Summary

This paper is a historical review of [[embodied_autonomous_intelligence]]. Its central claim is that a meaningful strand of robotics and artificial-agent research has treated intelligence not as a collection of isolated functions, but as an integrated property of a living or quasi-living embodied system.

The paper is especially interested in survival and homeostasis. Rather than designing agents around explicit external goals only, it highlights architectures whose primary objective is to maintain viable internal states. That shifts the framing from task completion to organism-like persistence.

## Framing

The review contrasts two broad traditions:

- Modular work targeting specific functions such as planning, perception, emotion, or language.
- Holistic work, from classical cybernetics onward, that treats intelligence as emerging from the coupling of body, environment, and internal regulation.

The paper argues that homeostasis is a recurring core idea in the second tradition. In these systems, the agent's behavior is grounded in maintaining internal balance rather than merely optimizing a predefined external reward.

## Relevance

For this vault, the paper is useful as a conceptual backdrop for reading autonomy research through embodiment and internal regulation. It complements [[cognitive_navigation]] by adding a survival-centered perspective: not just perception-decision-execution, but perception-decision-execution in service of maintaining the agent as an ongoing embodied system.

That is not a standard autonomous-racing objective, but it matters for how we think about robust autonomy. Racing systems are usually engineered around lap time, safety, and feasibility constraints; embodied-autonomy work asks what happens when the agent's internal viability becomes the first principle.

## Notes / Critique

The strength of the paper is the historical throughline. It connects cybernetics, robotics, and biologically inspired AI in a way that helps explain why homeostasis keeps reappearing in embodied intelligence research.

Its limitation, for this vault, is distance from the racing domain. The paper is a broad conceptual review rather than a racing-specific algorithm or system report.
