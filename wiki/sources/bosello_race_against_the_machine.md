# Bosello Race Against the Machine Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Bosello_Race_Against_the_Machine_A_Fully-Annotated_Open-Design_Dataset_of_Autonomous_and_Piloted_High-Speed_Flight.pdf`
> Paper: Race Against the Machine: A Fully-Annotated, Open-Design Dataset of Autonomous and Piloted High-Speed Flight, IEEE RA-L 2024

## Summary

This paper introduces an open dataset for high-speed flight that includes both autonomous and human-piloted runs.

For this vault, it matters because high-speed embodied autonomy often suffers from a lack of public data that is both physically realistic and richly annotated. This paper directly targets that gap for aggressive aerial flight.

## Method

The paper presents a fully annotated, open-design dataset for high-speed flight in a robotics context.

Its framing matters more than a single algorithmic novelty. By combining autonomous and piloted flight data, the dataset can support perception, learning, benchmarking, imitation, and human-versus-autonomy comparison in the same aggressive domain.

## Evaluation

The paper appears in IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters and is explicitly tagged as both aerial autonomy and robotics-vision dataset work.

For this vault, the key significance is infrastructure: strong high-speed systems need better data, and this paper contributes exactly that kind of public substrate.

## Notes / Critique

The strongest contribution is openness with annotation quality. High-speed flight is usually difficult to study because the relevant data are expensive to collect, hard to synchronize, and often kept closed.
