# Dupoux Why AI Systems Don't Learn Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Dupoux_Why_Ai_systems_Dont_Learn.pdf`
> Paper: Why AI systems don't learn and what to do about it: Lessons on autonomous learning from cognitive science

## Summary

This source is a conceptual critique of current AI learning paradigms. Its main claim is that contemporary AI systems often optimize effectively without truly `learning` in the richer autonomous sense suggested by cognitive science.

That makes it a very good fit for the vault. It speaks directly to the tension already visible between:

- the [[era_of_experience]] branch, which argues that experience must become the main substrate of capability,
- and the [[foundation_models_for_intelligent_decision_making]] branch, which still carries many assumptions inherited from static pretraining.

## Method / Framing

The framing is diagnostic rather than benchmark-driven.

The paper asks why present-day AI systems fail to learn autonomously in a robust, open-ended, self-directed way, and then turns to cognitive science for clues about what is missing.

From the local PDF structure, the core throughline appears to be:

- define what `autonomous learning` should mean,
- explain why present systems fall short,
- and sketch a path forward grounded in developmental and cognitive-science lessons rather than only scaling recipes.

## Relevance

This source is especially relevant to:

- [[autonomous_learning]]

It also matters as an interpretive counterpart to [[welcome_to_the_era_of_experience]]. Silver and Sutton say the field is moving toward experience. Dupoux, LeCun, and Malik sharpen the objection that current systems still do not truly learn autonomously in the way such a future would require.

## Notes / Critique

The source is valuable because it gives the vault a more serious critique than a simple `we need more data` story. It asks whether present AI systems are missing structural ingredients of learning itself.

Its limitation, at least from the local ingest, is that it is a framing document rather than a directly testable autonomy method. So its value is conceptual and architectural, not performance-driven.
