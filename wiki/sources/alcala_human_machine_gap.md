## Summary

This paper studies the remaining performance gap between machine racing systems and human drivers in car racing.

For this vault, it matters because it does not merely benchmark one stack against one human. It frames the broader `human-machine gap` itself as the object of study and asks which racing-performance dimensions still separate autonomous systems from human drivers.

## Evaluation

The metadata supports placing the paper as a comparative-analysis entry in the human-versus-machine racing branch.

The broader takeaway is that autonomous racing maturity should be judged not only by isolated wins or lap records, but by the structure of the remaining gap against humans across several racing-performance dimensions.
