# Firoozi Foundation Models in Robotics Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Firoozi_Foundation_Models_in_Robotics.pdf`
> Paper: Foundation models in robotics: Applications, challenges, and the future, IJRR 2025

## Summary

This paper surveys applications of pretrained foundation models across robotics and asks how they may change perception, planning, decision-making, and control.

For this vault, it matters because it is a broad robotics survey with a strong applications-and-challenges framing. It sits neatly between very abstract foundation-model arguments and narrower real-world deployment reviews.

## Method

The paper is a survey of foundation-model use in robotics.

Its organizing perspective is application-oriented: where pretrained models help robot autonomy, what forms of generalization they appear to enable, and which practical bottlenecks still prevent smooth transfer into embodied systems.

That makes it especially useful as a middle-layer reference. It is broader than a single real-robot deployment review, but more concrete than a purely strategic meta-analysis of general-purpose robotics.

## Evaluation

The paper's main contribution is synthesis. It surveys applications, challenges, and future directions for foundation models in robotics.

For this vault, the important value is that it offers a balanced robotics-centered map of the field: enough breadth to organize the space, but enough practical focus to keep the discussion tied to real autonomy stacks.

## Notes / Critique

The strongest contribution is scope with engineering relevance. The survey helps explain why foundation models are attractive in robotics while still foregrounding issues such as data mismatch, safety, embodiment, and real-time execution.
