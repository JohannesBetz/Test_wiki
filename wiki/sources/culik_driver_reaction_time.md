# Culik Driver Reaction Time Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Culik_Driver_Reaction_Time.pdf`
> Paper: Evaluation of Driver Reaction Time, Sensors, 2022

## Summary

This paper evaluates driver reaction time in a driving-simulator setting, with particular attention to younger drivers, driving experience, gender, and alcohol effects. Its main value is as a human-performance reference point rather than as an autonomy method paper.

For this vault, it is useful because highly dynamic autonomous systems are often discussed in terms of low reaction time and rapid closed-loop response, but human reaction limits are rarely made explicit. This paper helps anchor that part of the comparison.

## Core Contribution

The study uses simulator-based experiments to test how quickly drivers respond under several conditions, including expected and unexpected obstacles and alcohol-impaired driving scenarios.

That makes it relevant less for motorsport specifically than for the broader question:

- what reaction-time constraints shape human driving performance,
- and how should those limits be interpreted when comparing human and autonomous systems?

## Notes / Critique

This is a road-safety and simulator-human-factors paper, not a racing or robotics paper. Its value is therefore contextual and comparative, not algorithmic.
