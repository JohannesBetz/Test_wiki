# Huang FMs Challenges and Perspectives Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Huang_FMs_Challanges_and_perspectives.pdf`
> Paper: Foundation Models and Intelligent Decision-Making: Progress, Challenges, and Perspectives, The Innovation 2025

## Summary

This paper surveys intelligent decision-making (`IDM`) and asks how recent foundation models might reshape it. Rather than focusing on one robotics or driving domain, it traces a broader transition from rule-based and shallow-data-driven decision systems toward multimodal foundation-model-based decision architectures.

For this vault, the paper is useful as adjacent context for how navigation, control, and embodied autonomy may evolve once decision systems can draw on large multimodal priors instead of only narrow task-specific pipelines.

## Method

The survey reviews the historical progression of decision-making systems:

- rule-based and expert-system approaches,
- machine-learning and deep-reinforcement-learning approaches,
- and newer foundation-model-based paradigms.

It frames decision-making around environment, agents, rewards or utility, and intelligent tools, then discusses how foundation models can support perception, reasoning, adaptation, and cross-domain transfer in more general decision systems.

## Evaluation

As a review paper, its main contribution is synthesis rather than benchmark performance. The paper highlights both promise and friction points: multimodal foundation models can unify vision, language, and sensory data for more adaptive decisions, but practical deployment still depends on security, privacy, ethics, reliability, and efficient adaptation.

The survey also treats DRL as an important predecessor and component technology rather than as the endpoint of modern intelligent decision-making.

## Notes / Critique

The strongest contribution is scope. The paper gives a big-picture account of how decision systems may move from narrow optimization or RL pipelines toward more unified multimodal agents.

Its main limitation for this vault is abstraction level. It is more useful for strategic framing than for concrete racing or robotics implementation details.
