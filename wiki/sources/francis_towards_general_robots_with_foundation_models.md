# Francis Towards General Robots With Foundation Models Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Francis_Towards_General_Robots_With_FM.pdf`
> Paper: Towards General-Purpose Robots via Foundation Models: A Survey and Meta-Analysis, arXiv 2024

## Summary

This paper surveys the use of foundation models as a route toward more general-purpose robots.

For this vault, it matters because it sits one level above task-specific robotic deployments. Instead of focusing only on whether a particular foundation-model robot works in practice, it asks what the whole field is learning about generality, transfer, embodiment, and system design.

## Method

The paper is a survey and meta-analysis. Its lens is broader than a single application area: it studies how foundation models are being used across robotic settings and what that says about the path toward more general robots.

That makes it complementary to deployment-centered reviews. Rather than only cataloging successful real-world uses, it helps organize the conceptual landscape around what kinds of robotic generality current foundation-model methods are really achieving.

## Evaluation

The paper's main contribution is synthesis. It acts as a field map for the claim that foundation models may help robotics move from narrow task-specific systems toward more reusable and transferable agents.

For this vault, the important value is strategic: it helps connect embodied robotics, real-world deployment, and broader foundation-model ambition inside one narrative.

## Notes / Critique

The strongest contribution is scope. It keeps the vault's robotics-foundation-model branch from being only a collection of isolated papers by offering a higher-level account of where the field thinks robotic generality may come from.

It pairs especially well with [[real_world_robot_applications_of_foundation_models_a_review]], which is more grounded in physical deployment constraints.
