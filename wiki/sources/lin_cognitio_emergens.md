# Lin Cognitio Emergens Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Lin_Cognito_Emergens.pdf`
> Paper: Cognitio Emergens: Agency, Dimensions, and Dynamics in Human-AI Knowledge Co-Creation

## Summary

This source frames human-AI interaction not mainly as assistance, replacement, or narrow alignment, but as a process of `knowledge co-creation`.

For this vault, that matters because it strengthens the conceptual side of the `human-AI collaboration` branch. The paper asks how agency is distributed across humans and AI systems when new knowledge is produced jointly rather than merely retrieved, summarized, or executed.

## Method / Framing

The local PDF reads like a conceptual and analytical paper rather than a robotics benchmark or autonomy method.

Its key framing appears to revolve around:

- `agency` in human-AI interaction,
- the dimensions along which co-creation can vary,
- and the dynamics by which knowledge emerges through collaboration rather than unilateral generation.

That makes it a useful bridge between the vault's current `human-AI collaboration` branch and the broader `foundation-model` branch, especially where the question is no longer only what a model knows, but how humans and models jointly shape understanding.

## Relevance

This source is especially relevant to:

It also complements [[ai_and_human_co_improvement_for_safer_co_superintelligence]] nicely. Weston and Foerster focus on long-run co-improvement and safer co-superintelligence. Lin focuses more directly on the internal structure of collaborative knowledge production itself.

## Notes / Critique

The strength of the source is that it gives the vault a more granular vocabulary for thinking about human-AI collaboration. Instead of treating collaboration as a vague good, it asks what kind of agency distribution and knowledge dynamic is actually taking place.

Its limitation, from the vault's perspective, is that it is conceptual rather than operational. It sharpens how we think about co-creation, but it does not directly provide an evaluation method for fast robotics, autonomy, or embodied decision-making.
