# Lamparth Human vs Machine Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Lamparth_HumanvsMachine.pdf`
> Paper: Human vs. Machine: Behavioral Differences between Expert Humans and Language Models in Wargame Simulations, AIES 2024

## Summary

This paper compares expert human decision-making to large-language-model behavior in military-style wargame simulations. The core question is whether LLMs behave similarly enough to expert humans that they could plausibly support or replace strategic human judgment in high-stakes escalation scenarios.

For this vault, the paper is useful as adjacent context on strategic decision-making, model behavior under conflict scenarios, and the risks of using foundation models for recommendation or autonomy in high-stakes domains.

## Method

The paper uses a new wargame experiment based on a fictional US-China crisis scenario. The human side of the study includes 107 national security experts organized into teams. The model side simulates equivalent teams using GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 under different prompting and role-playing setups.

The analysis compares action choices, escalation tendencies, sensitivity to scenario treatments, and whether the LLMs can meaningfully emulate distinct human player traits or team discussion dynamics.

## Evaluation

The paper reports a significant high-level overlap between human and LLM responses, but also important qualitative and quantitative differences.

In particular, the LLM-simulated teams can be more aggressive, more sensitive to some framing changes, and poor at reproducing realistic internal disagreement. The simulated team discussions are described as artificially harmonious and lacking the quality of real strategic debate.

## Notes / Critique

The strongest contribution is that the comparison is grounded in an actual human-expert dataset rather than only in toy prompts or anecdotal judgments.

The main limitation is domain specificity. The study says something important about LLM behavior in strategic conflict simulations, but not necessarily about all decision tasks or all model architectures.
