# Weston AI and Human Co-Improvement Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Weston_AI_and_Human_Co_Improvement.pdf`
> Paper: AI & Human Co-Improvement for Safer Co-Superintelligence

## Summary

This source argues for a future in which AI and humans improve together rather than AI merely replacing human cognition or humans only supervising static systems from above.

For this vault, the paper matters because it expands the `human-AI collaboration` branch from narrow assistance and complementarity toward a stronger long-term framing:

**if systems become much more capable, safety may depend on whether humans and AI co-improve rather than drift into an adversarial replacement dynamic.**

## Method / Framing

The local PDF reads like a strategic and conceptual paper rather than an embodied-autonomy benchmark. Its references and framing suggest the authors are situating co-improvement against:

- self-improvement and agentic-AI narratives,
- preparedness and AI-safety discussions,
- and human-centered design traditions.

That makes it a useful bridge between the vault's current `human-AI collaboration`, `foundation-model`, and `AGI` branches.

## Relevance

This source is especially relevant to:

It also complements [[human_versus_artificial_intelligence]] nicely. Korteling asks how human and artificial intelligence differ and complement one another. Weston and Foerster push that one step further by asking how the two might improve each other under very high capability.

## Notes / Critique

The strength of the source is that it resists a simplistic `AI replaces humans` framing.

Its limitation, from the vault's perspective, is that it is strategic rather than operational. It does not directly tell us how to build a racing controller, a robot, or a grounded learning system. Instead, it reshapes the long-term governance and architecture question around co-improvement.
