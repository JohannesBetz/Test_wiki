# Modi Competitive Chess Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Modi_The_Effects_of_Computer_and_AI_Engines_on_Competit.pdf`
> Paper: The Effects of Computer and AI Engines on Competitive Chess, Journal of Student Research, 2023

## Summary

This paper discusses how chess engines and AI systems have changed competitive chess. Its core argument is that systems such as Deep Blue, Stockfish, and AlphaZero have clearly raised the level of play, but they have also changed the culture of preparation, creativity, and strategic individuality in the game.

For this vault, the useful value is not robotics or racing directly. It is a compact adjacent case study in how strong machine intelligence reshapes a competitive domain that used to be treated as a showcase for human expertise.

## Main Arc

The paper contrasts older human-centered chess culture with the modern engine era. It frames engines as both:

- performance amplifiers that improve analysis, preparation, and overall play quality,
- and pressure sources that can push competition toward memorization and engine dependence.

The paper's conclusion is not anti-AI. It argues for a hybrid stance in which engines remain powerful tools, but human judgment and creativity should still anchor competitive play.
