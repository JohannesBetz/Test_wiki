# Zilberman AGI Imagined Source

> Source: `raw/pdfs/Zibermann_AGI imagined.pdf`
> Paper: AGI imagined: how is AGI configured by the theories of mind, AI & Society, 2025

## Summary

This paper is a conceptual study of how different theories of mind shape the way artificial general intelligence is imagined. Its key move is not to ask whether AGI is already technically close, but to ask what researchers mean by AGI when they inherit different assumptions about what a mind is.

For this vault, the paper is useful as conceptual scaffolding at the boundary between [[foundation_models_for_intelligent_decision_making]], [[embodied_autonomous_intelligence]], and broader AGI discourse.

## Core Contrast

The paper foregrounds a tension between at least two mind pictures:

- a more computational or representational theory of mind,
- and an embodied-cognition view in which mind depends more deeply on body, environment, and situated interaction.

That contrast matters because each framing implies a different picture of what AGI would need in order to count as genuinely general or genuinely intelligent.

## Why It Matters Here

The vault increasingly circles around the same underlying question from different directions:

- Can intelligence be grounded mostly in pretrained symbolic structure?
- Must it be grounded in embodied interaction and experience?
- How much of general intelligence is reasoning, and how much is closed-loop situated adaptation?

This paper helps make those disagreements explicit rather than leaving them as background assumptions.

## Notes / Critique

This is a framing paper, not an algorithmic one. That means it is better for clarifying research assumptions than for extracting implementation guidance.

Still, that kind of clarification is useful in this vault, because many later disagreements about foundation models, experience, embodiment, and autonomy are really disagreements about what sort of "mind" an intelligent system must have.
