# AGI imagined: how is AGI configured by the theories of mind

> AGI imagined: how is AGI configured by the theories of mind | AI & Society | Jacob Zilberman

## Summary

This paper asks how visions of artificial general intelligence are shaped by underlying theories of mind. Instead of treating AGI as a purely technical scaling target, it argues that different assumptions about what minds are lead to different assumptions about what AGI should look like.

For this vault, the paper matters because it makes explicit a tension already present across many pages: whether intelligence is best understood as symbolic-computational competence, as embodied situated interaction, or as some hybrid of both.

## Core Idea

The paper contrasts computational theories of mind with embodied-cognition-oriented views. In the computational framing, AGI is more naturally imagined as a system that manipulates representations and reasons over them at broad scale.

In the embodied-cognition framing, AGI cannot be separated so cleanly from body, environment, action, and lived interaction. Intelligence then looks less like detached abstract reasoning and more like situated adaptive sense-making.

That difference matters because it changes what researchers think they are building when they talk about AGI.

## Notes / Critique

The strongest contribution is conceptual clarity. It makes hidden assumptions visible and therefore helps explain why AGI discussions can sound aligned while actually referring to very different end states.

Its main limitation for this vault is practical distance. It is a framing paper, not a control, planning, learning, or deployment paper, so its value is mostly in sharpening how we interpret the more technical branches.
