# Abu Dhabi Autonomous Racing League

## Definition

The Abu Dhabi Autonomous Racing League, or A2RL, is a full-scale autonomous racing competition using standardized race vehicles and multi-team autonomous driving software stacks.

## Relevance

[[challenging_f1_drivers_in_physical_race_cars_with_human_inspired_online_learning]] uses A2RL 2025 as a major physical validation setting for [[apex]]. TUM Autonomous Motorsport's vehicle Hailey competes on the Yas Marina North layout against both autonomous competitors and Formula 1 driver Daniil Kvyat.

[[a_multi_stage_time_variant_motion_planner_for_agile_autonomous_driving_maneuvers]] evaluates dynamic obstacle avoidance and overtaking scenarios on Yas Marina Circuit in an A2RL-inspired setting.

[[model_structured_neural_networks_to_control_the_steering_dynamics_of_autonomous_race_cars]] uses real telemetry from the 2024 A2RL competition to train and validate a model-structured neural steering controller for a full-scale autonomous race car.

## Notes

The A2RL validation is important because it evaluates online learning under race-event conditions: tire-temperature changes, yellow/red flags, traffic, overtaking attempts, and multi-agent autonomous racing, rather than only controlled single-car time trials.
